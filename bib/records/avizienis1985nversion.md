METHOD (three sentences)
This is a review/position paper by the originator of NVP summarizing the UCLA Dependable Computing group's program of work (started 1975). It surveys the conceptual framework (multiple computation generalized to design diversity), reports on a series of experimental NVP studies (e.g., 27- and 16-program experiments, specification-technique studies), and describes the DEDIX distributed supervisor/testbed for executing N-version software. It does not present a single new controlled experiment; it consolidates definitions, requirements, prior empirical results, and research goals.

LIMITATIONS
The paper itself stresses the central threat to NVP: "similar errors" (coincident/correlated failures across versions) that can outvote correct results and defeat the independence assumption (Sec. II-III, pp. 1491-1492); faults are classified as related vs. independent and the author notes that determining whether two coincident faults share a common cause is unresolved "at the present stage of our understanding." As a review by the technique's inventor it is advocacy-leaning, and quantitative reliability gains are presented as hypotheses/expectations rather than firmly established.
