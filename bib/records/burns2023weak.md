CLAIM CARDS

1. Quote: "can we use weak models to supervise strong models? ... finetuning large (strong) pretrained models on labels generated by small (weak) models." (§1) -> Paraphrase: A weak supervisor's labels are used to train a stronger model.

2. Quote: "we finetune a strong model with the generated weak labels." (§3, Methodology) -> Paraphrase: The strong student learns from the weak teacher's (noisy) labels.

3. Quote: "strong pretrained models naturally generalize beyond their weak supervisors ... they consistently outperform their weak supervisors." (§4.2) -> Paraphrase: Weak-to-strong generalization recovers capability beyond the supervisor.

METHOD
As an empirical analogy for humans supervising superhuman models, the authors finetune strong GPT-4-family models on labels produced by weaker models across NLP benchmarks (22 datasets), chess puzzles, and reward modeling. They measure "performance gap recovered" (PGR) -- the fraction of the weak-to-strong-ceiling gap recovered -- and test enhancements like bootstrapping, an auxiliary confidence loss, and generative finetuning. Strong students generalize beyond weak supervisors but do not fully close the gap naively.

LIMITATIONS (authors' own)
"Naively finetuning on weak supervision is not enough" and "we are still far from recovering the full performance gap." Two disanalogies are flagged: imitation saliency (future models may more easily imitate human errors) and pretraining leakage (human-level knowledge is already in pretraining, unlike latent superhuman knowledge).
